# OG Image Creation Instructions

You still need to create an **OG Image** (Open Graph image) for social media sharing.

## What is an OG Image?

When someone shares your website on Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, etc., this image appears in the preview.

## Specifications:

- **Size:** 1200 x 630 pixels (exact)
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **File size:** Under 300 KB
- **Filename:** `og-image.jpg`
- **Location:** `frontend/public/og-image.jpg`

## Design Tips:

1. **Use your brand colors** (blue/indigo based on your site)
2. **Keep text large** - Minimum 60px font size
3. **High contrast** - Dark text on light background or vice versa
4. **Include your logo** - Top or center
5. **Add key benefits** - What you offer
6. **Include domain** - So people know where it goes
7. **Test on mobile** - Will be viewed on small screens too

## Tools to Use:

### Option 1: Canva (Easiest)
1. Go to [Canva.com](https://canva.com)
2. Search for "Facebook Post" or "Open Graph" template
3. Resize to 1200 x 630 pixels
4. Add your text and logo
5. Download as JPG

### Option 2: Figma (Professional)
1. Create new frame: 1200 x 630
2. Add your design elements
3. Export as JPG at 2x quality

### Option 3: Photoshop/GIMP
1. New document: 1200 x 630 pixels
2. Design your image
3. Save for web as JPG (quality 80-85%)

## Quick Template:

If you want a simple design:

**Background:** Solid color or subtle gradient (your brand blue)
**Logo:** Top center, 200px wide
**Headline:** "Military Disability Nexus" - 72px, bold, white
**Subheadline:** "Expert VA Medical Documentation" - 48px, white
**Bullets:** 4 key services - 36px, white
**Domain:** Bottom right - 24px, white/80% opacity

## After Creating:

1. Save as `og-image.jpg`
2. Place in `frontend/public/`
3. Commit and push
4. Test with:
   - [Facebook Debugger](https://developers.facebook.com/tools/debug/)
   - [Twitter Card Validator](https://cards-dev.twitter.com/validator)
