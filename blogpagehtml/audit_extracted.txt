2.3 GEO & Answer Engine Optimization
Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is about making your content the source that AI chatbots cite. Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) focuses on getting your content into featured snippets and AI Overviews. Here’s what to do:
Write direct, authoritative answers in the first 2-3 sentences of each FAQ. AI engines pull from concise, definitional content.
Use the exact question phrasing veterans type (“What is a nexus letter?” “Do I need a DBQ for my VA claim?”) as your H3/H4 headings.
Add “Quick Answer” or “Short Answer” blocks at the top of FAQ answers before diving into details.
Include statistics and cite authoritative sources (VA.gov, 38 CFR sections, BVA case law) to increase citation trustworthiness.
Ensure E-E-A-T signals are strong: author bios on blog posts, clinician credentials on service pages, medical review policy (you have this — good), and publication dates.

4. FAQ Section Optimization (All Pages)
This is the single highest-impact change you can make for GEO and AEO. Currently, your FAQ sections across all service pages show question headings but answers are hidden behind JavaScript accordions. AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, Google AI Overviews) and search engine crawlers often cannot access accordion-hidden content.
Universal FAQ Fixes
Render all FAQ answers as visible HTML text. You can still use accordions visually, but the answer text must exist in the page source (use CSS to collapse/expand rather than JavaScript that injects content).
Implement FAQPage schema (JSON-LD) on every page with FAQs. This enables rich snippets in Google search and makes your answers eligible for AI citation.
Structure each answer with a direct 1-2 sentence answer first, followed by detailed explanation. AI engines preferentially cite the first sentence of an answer block.
Use the exact natural-language question phrasing that veterans search for.
Add 3-5 new FAQ questions per page targeting long-tail keywords.

7.3 Page Content Outline
H1: TDIU (Total Disability Individual Unemployability) Medical Documentation
Subheadline: Expert medical opinions and vocational evidence to support your TDIU claim when service-connected disabilities prevent gainful employment.
Section 1: What is TDIU?
Define TDIU clearly: a VA benefit that compensates veterans at the 100% rate when service-connected disabilities prevent them from maintaining substantially gainful employment, even though their combined rating is less than 100%. Explain the two pathways: Schedular TDIU (one condition rated 60%+ or combined 70%+ with one at 40%+) and Extraschedular TDIU (doesn’t meet schedular thresholds but still can’t work). Cite 38 CFR §4.16.
Section 2: How Medical Documentation Supports TDIU
Explain that TDIU requires proving that specific service-connected conditions (not age, not non-service-connected conditions) prevent the veteran from working. Describe what your clinicians provide: a detailed medical opinion addressing functional limitations, the impact of service-connected conditions on the ability to perform physical and sedentary work, and how symptoms like pain, fatigue, cognitive impairment, or psychiatric symptoms create occupational impairment.
Section 3: What’s Included
Comprehensive review of medical records, VA rating decisions, and employment history
Medical opinion on functional limitations caused by service-connected disabilities
Assessment of how conditions impact physical AND sedentary occupational capacity
Opinion addressing whether the veteran can maintain substantially gainful employment
VA-compliant language and evidence-based rationale with medical literature citations
Section 4: Who Qualifies for TDIU?
Detail the schedular and extraschedular criteria. Include a brief table showing the rating thresholds. Mention that veterans who are working but in marginal or protected employment may still qualify.
Section 5: Common Reasons TDIU Claims Get Denied
Medical evidence doesn’t link unemployability to service-connected conditions specifically
C&P exam focuses on symptom severity but doesn’t address occupational impact
Veteran is currently employed above the marginal employment threshold
Non-service-connected conditions are blamed for unemployment
Missing or insufficient vocational evidence

9.1 Rebuttal Letters / Addendum Opinions
Market demand: HIGH. 
When the VA conducts a C&P exam and the examiner provides a negative opinion that contradicts a veteran’s private nexus letter or downplays their symptoms, a rebuttal letter from the original clinician (or a new one) is the most effective counter-evidence. Multiple competitors (VA Claims Insider, VetNexusLetter, Telemedica) explicitly advertise rebuttal letters. This is a natural extension of your nexus letter service.
What to offer:
Clinician-authored rebuttal that addresses specific errors, omissions, or mischaracterizations in a VA C&P exam report
Point-by-point response to the C&P examiner’s rationale
Supplemental medical literature citations
Can be offered as a standalone service or as an add-on for existing nexus letter clients whose letters were subsequently challenged
Suggested pricing: 
$400–$750 depending on complexity
Suggested URL: 
/services/rebuttal-letter-cp-exam
9.2 Personal Statement / Buddy Statement Guidance
Market demand: MODERATE-HIGH. 
Lay statements from the veteran and their family/friends are important supporting evidence that many veterans struggle to write effectively. This service could include a guided template, a one-on-one coaching session to help the veteran articulate their symptoms and daily limitations in VA-language, and review/editing of draft statements. This pairs naturally with your Claim Readiness Review and C&P Exam Coaching services.
Suggested pricing: 
$75–$150 for template + review; can be bundled with CRR or C&P coaching
9.3 Vocational Assessment / Employability Evaluation
Market demand: HIGH (especially for TDIU). 
TDIU claims benefit enormously from a vocational expert’s assessment of the veteran’s ability to maintain gainful employment. Elite Medical Review Associates specifically promotes vocational expert nexus letters for TDIU. If you can partner with or hire a vocational rehabilitation counselor, this becomes a premium add-on to your TDIU documentation service.
Suggested pricing:

9.4 Independent Medical Examination (IME)
Market demand: MODERATE. 
Distinguished from an IMO (records-based opinion only), a full IME includes a clinical examination of the veteran — either via telehealth video or in-person. Dr. Willoughby & Associates positions their “psychological IME” as a premium alternative to basic nexus letters, with more clinical depth and higher evidentiary weight. This is especially relevant for mental health claims (PTSD, anxiety, depression) where a clinical interview adds significant probative value.
