# SEO Strategy

Date: 2026-04-08

## Executive Summary

This site is best positioned to win high-intent, long-tail SEO around:

- VA nexus letters and independent medical opinions
- private DBQs for VA disability claims
- C&P exam preparation and coaching
- 1151 medical evidence and claim support
- Aid and Attendance / VA Form 21-2680
- VA claim readiness and evidence checklists

The fastest path to rankings is not trying to win broad head terms first. The site should use:

- service pages as money pages
- blog posts as topic-cluster support
- case studies as proof assets
- community pages as long-tail question capture

Assumptions used in this plan:

- business goal is lead generation, not pure traffic
- target market is U.S. veterans nationwide
- no Google Search Console export or keyword-tool data was available in the workspace
- prioritization is based on current live/build URL inventory and commercial fit

## Business Type

National service business in a high-trust YMYL niche.

This means rankings will depend heavily on:

- topical authority
- evidence of real expertise
- proof content
- internal linking
- original, condition-specific pages that actually answer search intent

### Community Page Strategy

Use the community question template as a long-tail acquisition layer, not as the main place to target core commercial keywords.

Pattern to keep:

- question title closely matches the searched phrase
- every question page links to one relevant service page
- every question page links to one relevant blog or case study
- top-performing community questions should later become full expert articles

Current examples with strong long-tail potential:

- `my migraines seem to be triggered by my tinnitus, will VA consider that?`
- `my migraines wipe me out for hours does that count as a prostrating attack?`
- `i have a c&p exam coming up how do i make sure i dont undersell my symptoms?`

## What To Optimize Before Publishing More Content

1. Add a FAQ block to each money page with schema markup.
2. Add stronger internal links from every blog and case study to its supporting service page.
3. Add author/reviewer blocks, credentials, and last-updated dates to all educational pages.
4. Standardize service page titles so each page owns one clear primary keyword.
5. Add "related articles" and "related case studies" modules sitewide.
6. Add CTA blocks inside blog and case-study content, not just at the bottom.
7. Review all existing blog posts for depth, examples, citations, and condition-specific wording.
8. Use Search Console as soon as possible to validate which impressions already exist before writing low-priority topics.

## Internal Linking Rules

Every new article should link to:

- one main service page
- one supporting case study
- one related article
- one conversion page such as `/forms`, `/contact`, or `/diagnostic`

Every service page should link out to:

- 3 to 5 supporting articles
- 2 to 3 case studies
- 1 testimonial or trust page

## KPIs To Track

### First 90 Days

- 12 new high-quality pages published
- every main service page has at least 5 relevant internal links pointing in
- impressions increase for non-brand long-tail queries
- first-page movement for at least 3 to 5 long-tail target keywords

### 6 Months

- 10 to 20 non-brand keywords in top 20
- 5 to 10 high-intent queries in top 10
- measurable organic leads to `/forms`, `/contact`, `/diagnostic`, or service pages

### 12 Months

- one defensible content cluster around Nexus/IMO
- one defensible content cluster around C&P exam prep
- one defensible content cluster around 1151 and Aid and Attendance

## What Not To Do

- do not publish thin AI pages that say the same thing with different titles
- do not target the same keyword on multiple service pages
- do not rely only on generic broad terms like `nexus letter` or `VA disability help`
- do not leave blog posts disconnected from service pages
- do not publish new content without a clear internal-link target
